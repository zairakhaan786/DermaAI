Research & Methodology

Unveiling the technology and data science behind DermaAI's advanced dermatological diagnostic system.

Model Architecture

DermaAI utilizes the ResNet50 (Residual Network) architecture, a powerhouse in the world of Deep Learning and Computer Vision.

Why ResNet50?

Traditional deep networks suffer from the "vanishing gradient" problem as they get deeper. ResNet solves this using "skip connections" or residuals, allowing the network to learn identity mappings and go much deeper (up to 50 layers in our case) without losing performance. This depth is critical for capturing the subtle visual features of varied skin lesions.

Model Performance

Our model achieved a high validation accuracy during testing on balanced clinical datasets, maintaining a strong balance between sensitivity and specificity.

Dataset: HAM10000

The training was conducted on the HAM10000 ("Human Against Machine with 10000 training images") dataset. This is a large collection of multi-source dermatoscopic images of common pigmented skin lesions.

  • Diverse clinical cases from different populations
  • Ground truth established by histopathology or consensus
  • Covers 9 distinct diagnostic categories
