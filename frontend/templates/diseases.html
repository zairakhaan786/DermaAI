Skin Conditions We Detect

DermaAI is trained to classify 9 distinct skin conditions. Learn about each condition below.

Actinic Keratosis

Precancerous rough, scaly patches caused by prolonged exposure to sunlight. Early treatment is important to prevent progression to skin cancer.

Atopic Dermatitis

Chronic, itchy skin condition causing redness, dryness, and inflammation. Commonly known as eczema; often flares periodically.

Benign Keratosis

Non-cancerous skin growth, often rough, brown, or wart-like in appearance. Generally harmless but can sometimes be mistaken for melanoma.

Dermatofibroma

Firm, small, harmless skin nodule, usually found on the legs or arms. Often caused by a minor injury such as an insect bite.

Melanocytic Nevus

Commonly known as a mole; pigmented, benign growth from melanocytes. Should be monitored for any changes in size, shape, or color.

Melanoma

Serious skin cancer developing in melanocyte cells; often dark, irregular, and fast-growing. Early detection greatly improves survival rates.

Squamous Cell Carcinoma

Common form of skin cancer; appears as scaly, red patches or sores that may bleed or crust. Caused largely by UV radiation exposure.

Tinea Ringworm Candidiasis

Contagious ring-shaped rash caused by a skin-invading fungus. Spreads through direct contact and thrives in warm, moist conditions.

Vascular Lesion

Abnormal blood vessels appearing on or near the skin surface as red, purple, or blue marks. Includes port-wine stains and spider veins.
